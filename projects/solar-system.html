Project 02 — 3D Modeling & Animation

3D Solar System

A fully modeled and animated solar system built in Blender 3.6 LTS, featuring every planet, the Sun, and Pluto — with orbital motion, textures, and Python scripting.

Blender 3.6 LTS Python 3D Modeling EEVEE Render PBR Textures

Section 01

About the Project

This project is a 3D simulation of our solar system created entirely within Blender 3.6 LTS. The goal was to model each celestial body with accurate relative sizing, apply realistic PBR texture materials, and script the orbital motion programmatically using Python.

The project demonstrates the integration of artistic 3D modeling with technical scripting, producing a rendered animation that captures the scale and motion of the solar system. Submitted as a major output for the subject Multimedia and Authoring.

Project Details

Tools Used

  • Blender 3.6 LTS — primary 3D modeling and animation software
  • Python 3.11 — scripting orbital mechanics and automation
  • NASA texture maps — surface material references
  • EEVEE renderer — real-time rendering engine with bloom

Render Info

  • Renderer: EEVEE (GPU accelerated)
  • Resolution: 1920 × 1080 (1080p)
  • Frame rate: 24 FPS
  • Total frames: 2,941
  • Total render time: ~1 hour 15 minutes
  • Average per frame: ~1.55 sec
  • Output format: MP4 (H.264)

Key Features

  • All 8 planets + Pluto + Sun modeled and textured
  • Orbital motion automated via Python script
  • Axial tilt applied to each planet
  • Sun emission shader with volumetric glow
  • Saturn ring system with transparency texture
  • HDRI space environment background

Texture Materials

  • Sun — emission shader, noise-based surface texture
  • Earth — diffuse map, cloud layer, specular water map
  • Gas giants — procedural band textures (Jupiter, Saturn)
  • Ice giants — tinted emission + diffuse blend (Uranus, Neptune)
  • Rocky planets — bump-mapped crater surface textures
  • Saturn rings — alpha-mapped ring plane geometry

Texture Credits

  • Solar System Scope — primary planet surface maps
  • Planet Pixel Emporium — Earth surface, clouds, and bump maps
  • DeviantArt — community-created Europa and moon textures

Scene Breakdown

The final animation sequence as scripted in the Blender Python file. Total runtime: ~2 minutes at 24 FPS (2,941 frames).

Frame 1 – 240  |  0:00 – 0:10

Opening — Deep Space Approach

Camera starts at a distant vantage point and slowly approaches the solar system, establishing the HDRI star field and spatial scale.

Frame 241 – 410  |  0:10 – 0:17

Focus — The Sun

Camera orbits the Sun with a dedicated focus camera and two-point lighting, showcasing the emission shader, corona glow, and surface texture. Fading name label overlay.

Frame 411 – 580  |  0:17 – 0:24

Focus — Mercury

Close-up of Mercury's heavily cratered surface with bump-mapped textures. The smallest and innermost planet.

Frame 581 – 750  |  0:24 – 0:31

Focus — Venus

Venus shown with its thick sulfuric cloud layer texture, rotating slowly in its retrograde direction.

Frame 751 – 920  |  0:31 – 0:38

Focus — Earth

Earth with day map, cloud layer, and orbiting Moon. Axial tilt of 23.4° visible with atmospheric detail.

Frame 921 – 1090  |  0:38 – 0:45

Focus — Mars

Mars with surface and bump textures, accompanied by its two small moons Phobos and Deimos in orbit.

Frame 1091 – 1260  |  0:45 – 0:52

Focus — Jupiter

The gas giant with banded atmosphere, orbited by the four Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.

Frame 1261 – 1430  |  0:53 – 1:00

Focus — Saturn

Saturn and its textured ring system with moons Titan, Enceladus, Iapetus, Hyperion, and Phoebe.

Frame 1431 – 1600  |  1:00 – 1:07

Focus — Uranus

The sideways ice giant (98° tilt) with four faint ring bands and moons Titania, Oberon, and Ariel.

Frame 1601 – 1770  |  1:07 – 1:14

Focus — Neptune

Deep blue methane-rich atmosphere with three ring bands, and moons Triton and Nereid.

Frame 1771 – 1940  |  1:14 – 1:21

Focus — Pluto

Kuiper Belt dwarf planet with its binary companion Charon and four small moons: Nix, Hydra, Kerberos, and Styx.

Frame 1941 – 2941  |  1:21 – 2:02

Closing — Full System Wide Shot

Camera pulls back to reveal the entire solar system in motion. Orbital speed ramps up so Pluto completes one full revolution. All visible orbit paths and bodies animate simultaneously.
